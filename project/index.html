The Founding of Kisyombe Village

The Journey of Wayanga Sichali

From Chibula, Misuku Hills to Chiwondo Lagoon

Kisyombe Village traces its roots to Wayanga Sichali, a hunter from Chibula, Misuku Hills in Chitipa. Wayanga departed from Chibula on a hunting expedition and never returned for years. His grandfather, Chief Mwenemisuku, reported his disappearance to Paramount Chief Kyungu in Karonga.

Years later, Wayanga arrived at Chiwondo Lagoon in Karonga. Village women collecting firewood found him roasting wild game. Frightened by his appearance after years in the bush, they initially fled but later reported their discovery. Some men wanted to kill the stranger, but others convinced them to bring Wayanga to Chief Kyungu.

The Nkhomo Legacy at Mlare

"Bapyolite imbwa yane ni nkhomo" ‚Äî "They broke my dog with a stick."

Shalonda's younger brother was also a hunter. One day while hunting at Mlare, a place called Phughu, a native broke his dog's leg with a stick. He went to Kyungu Gwazapasi Kalambo (1850-1868) crying, saying "Bapyolite imbwa yane ni nkhomo".

Kyungu nicknamed him Nkhomo and appointed him as leader at Mphughu. Today, the Sichali clan has two major branches in Karonga:
