Chiondo Lagoon & Natural Features

Chiondo Lagoon

The Sacred Lagoon with Salty Water

Chiondo Lagoon is the heart of Kisyombe Village. This unique lagoon contains salty water due to having no outlet, creating a distinct ecosystem. The lagoon is named after the sacred snake Chiondo, which the people believe represents their god working hand in hand with Kisindire and Vilaure to protect Karonga.

The lagoon is both a spiritual site and a vital resource for fishing. Traditional regulations, overseen by the Mkwasi priesthood, ensure sustainable use and respect for its sacred nature.

Sacred Beliefs of Chiondo

Chiondo - The Sacred Snake

The god represented by a big snake residing in the lagoon. Offerings of white chicken are made during calamities.

Kisindire

Spiritual partner of Chiondo, working together to protect the people of Karonga.

Vilaure

Guardian spirits that work with Chiondo and Kisindire for community protection.

Mkwasi Priesthood

The Mkwasi clan serves as traditional priests, leading Chikungu ceremonies and sacrifices.

Fishing Livelihoods

Chiondo Lagoon Fishing

Subsistence fishing in the sacred lagoon. The salty water creates unique fish species. Traditional fishing methods are practiced with respect for the lagoon's spiritual significance.

Lake Malawi Beaches

Kisyombe Village has access to beautiful beaches along Lake Malawi. Fishing in the lake provides both food and income for many families, particularly from the Ndovie/Phiri clan.
