Chiwondo Lagoon has no outlet channel, causing water to be trapped and increasing salinity through evaporation. This geological feature is rare in Malawi.

Sacred Beliefs of Chiwondo

The Chiwondo Spirit

The people of Kisyombe believe in a god represented by a big snake called Chiwondo that resides in the lagoon. This spiritual entity works hand in hand with Kisindire and Vilaure to provide protection, healing, and prosperity to the people of Karonga under the authority of the Kyungu Dynasty.

Sacrifices and Offerings

During times of calamity‚Äîdrought, disease, or misfortune‚Äîthe community gathers at Chiwondo Lagoon to offer sacrifice. A white chicken is offered by the Mkwasi clan priest while prayers are made for protection and healing. This tradition has been maintained for centuries under the authority of successive Kyungu chiefs.

Chikungu Ceremony

At the beginning of the rainy season, the community performs the Chikungu ceremony to ensure good harvests. A member of the Mkwasi family leads these ceremonies as the traditional priest, a practice dating back to the early Kyungu period. The ceremony includes prayers, offerings, and community feasting.

Traditional representation of Chiwondo, the sacred snake

Fishing Livelihoods

üé£ Chiwondo Lagoon Fishing

Subsistence fishing in the sacred lagoon provides protein and income for the community. The salty water creates unique fish species not found in freshwater lakes. Traditional fishing methods include:

  • Basket traps (mitolo)
  • Hook and line (ndoza)
  • Small gill nets (mauwa)

Clan: Ndovie/Phiri (primary fishing clan)

Season: Year-round, with restrictions during breeding

  • Open water fishing
  • Beach seining (koka)
  • Fish processing and smoking

Main catch: Utaka, Chambo, Mlamba (catfish)

Conservation & Preservation

Chiwondo Lagoon faces threats from climate change, invasive species, and changing rainfall patterns. The community, under the authority of Paramount Chief Kyungu and Village Head Kisyombe, is working to preserve this sacred site through:

  • Traditional fishing regulations (closed seasons, gear restrictions)
  • Buffer zone protection around the lagoon
  • Documentation of sacred knowledge by Mkwasi elders
  • Educational programs at Chiwondo Primary School

Chiwondo Primary School

Named after the sacred lagoon

Chiwondo Primary School has educated generations of Kisyombe children. Named after the sacred lagoon, the school represents the community's commitment to education while honoring traditional heritage. Many graduates have become teachers, nurses, agricultural officers, and business leaders who contribute to Karonga and Malawi's economy.
