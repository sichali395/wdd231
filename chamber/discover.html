About Karonga

History

Karonga is a town in the Karonga District in Northern Region of Malawi. Located on the western shore of Lake Malawi, it was established as a British colonial post in the late 19th century.

Culture

The town is home to various ethnic groups including the Tumbuka, Nkhonde, and Tonga people, each with their own unique traditions, dances, and cultural practices.

Economy

Karonga's economy is based on agriculture (rice, cotton, maize), fishing in Lake Malawi, and mining (uranium at Kayelekera Mine). Tourism is also growing.

Climate

Karonga has a tropical savanna climate with distinct wet (November-April) and dry (May-October) seasons. Temperatures average 25-30°C year-round.
