Sacred Beliefs of Chiwondo

The Chiwondo Spirit

The people of Kisyombe believe in a god represented by a big snake called Chiwondo that resides in the lagoon. This spiritual entity works hand in hand with Kisindire and Vilaure to provide protection, healing, and prosperity to the people of Karonga under the authority of the Kyungu Dynasty.

Sacrifices and Offerings

During times of calamity, the community gathers at Chiwondo Lagoon to offer sacrifice. A white chicken is offered by the Mkwasi clan priest while prayers are made for protection and healing.

Chikungu Ceremony

At the beginning of the rainy season, the community performs the Chikungu ceremony to ensure good harvests. A member of the Mkwasi family leads these ceremonies as the traditional priest.

Traditional representation of Chiwondo, the sacred snake

Conservation & Preservation

Chiwondo Lagoon faces threats from climate change. The community, under Paramount Chief Kyungu, is working to preserve this sacred site through:

  • Traditional fishing regulations
  • Buffer zone protection
  • Documentation of sacred knowledge
  • Educational programs at Chiwondo School
