The Founding of Kisyombe Village

The Journey of Wayanga Sichali

Kisyombe Village traces its roots to Wayanga Sichali, a hunter from Chibula, Misuku Hills in Chitipa. Wayanga departed from Chibula on a hunting expedition and never returned for years. His grandfather, Chief Mwenemisuku, reported his disappearance to Paramount Chief Kyungu in Karonga.

Years later, Wayanga arrived at Chiondo Lagoon in Karonga. One day, village women collecting firewood found him roasting wild game. Frightened by his appearance after years in the bush, they initially fled but later reported their discovery. Some men wanted to kill the stranger, but others convinced them to bring Wayanga to Chief Kyungu.

Chief Kyungu recognized Wayanga and appointed him as village headman, with Mwamnkhasi Mwachunda as his bodyguard. Kyungu also gave Wayanga a wife named Kasyombe, whose name became the title of leadership in the village.

Lineage of Leadership:

  • Wayanga Sichali - First leader, appointed by Kyungu
  • Shalonda Sichali - Son of Wayanga, inherited the title "Kisyombe"
  • Nkhomo Sichali - Younger brother, appointed leader at Mphughu, Mlare
  • Current Kisyombe - Direct descendant of Shalonda

The Nkhomo Legacy at Mlare

Shalonda's younger brother was also a hunter. One day while hunting at Mlare, a place called Phughu, a native broke his dog's leg with a stick. He went to Chief Kyungu crying, saying "Bapyolite imbwa yane ni nkhomo" ‚Äî "They broke my dog with a stick."

Chief Kyungu nicknamed him Nkhomo and appointed him as leader at Mphughu. Today, the Sichali clan has two major branches in Karonga:

Kisyombe Branch

Based at Chiondo Lagoon, Kisyombe Village

Title: Kisyombe

Nkhomo Branch

Based at Mlare, Mphughu

Title: Nkhomo

Sacred Beliefs: Chiondo, Kisindire & Vilaure

Chiondo - The Sacred Snake

The people of Kisyombe believe in a god represented by a big snake called Chiondo, who resides in Chiondo Lagoon. Chiondo works hand in hand with Kisindire and Vilaure to protect the people of Karonga.

Sacrifices to Chiondo

During times of calamity, the community offers sacrifice to Chiondo in the form of a white chicken. This ceremony is led by a priest from the Mkwasi family.

Chikungu Ceremony

At the beginning of the rainy season, the community performs the Chikungu ceremony to ensure good harvests. A member of the Mkwasi family leads these ceremonies as the traditional priest.

Chiondo Primary School

Named after the sacred lagoon

Chiondo Primary School has educated generations of Kisyombe children. Many graduates have gone on to become teachers, nurses, agricultural officers, and business leaders who contribute to the economy of Kisyombe, Karonga, and Malawi as a whole.

The school stands as a testament to the community's commitment to education while preserving cultural heritage.
